Tech failures are inevitable. But panic doesn't have to be.

- **Backup plans** so you never lose a lesson to tech failure

**Examples:**
- Digital presentation? Print key slides.

- Video lesson? Have discussion questions ready.

## The 10 Most Common Tech Problems (And Fast Fixes)

### Problem 1: Projector Won't Turn On

**Symptoms:** No light, no image, no response from remote

**Quick Fixes (Try in this order):**

1. **Check the power cable** - Is it plugged in? Is the outlet working? (Sounds obvious, but it's often the culprit.)

2. **Try the manual power button** - The remote might be dead. Look for a physical button on the projector itself.

3. **Wait 2 minutes, try again** - Some projectors have a cooldown period. If someone just used it, it might need a rest.

4. **Check the lamp** - If the projector is old, the bulb might be dead. Look for a flashing red light (often means lamp failure).

**If none of this works:**
- Use your backup plan (printed slides, whiteboard, document camera)
- Submit IT ticket: "Projector in Room [X] not powering on. Checked power cable and manual button. Need replacement bulb or service."

---

### Problem 2: Projector is On But Screen is Blank

**Symptoms:** Projector light is on, but no image on screen

**Quick Fixes:**

1. **Check the input source** - Press "Source" or "Input" button on remote. Cycle through HDMI 1, HDMI 2, VGA, etc. Your laptop might be on a different input.

2. **Check your laptop display settings** - On Windows: Press `Windows + P`, choose "Duplicate" or "Extend." On Mac: System Preferences ‚Üí Displays ‚Üí Detect Displays.

3. **Reconnect the HDMI/VGA cable** - Unplug and replug both ends. Sometimes it's just a loose connection.

4. **Try a different cable** - If you have a spare HDMI or VGA cable, swap it out.

**If none of this works:**
- Use your backup plan
- IT ticket: "Projector in Room [X] not displaying image. Checked input source and cables. Laptop display settings correct. Need troubleshooting."

---

### Problem 3: No WiFi Connection

**Symptoms:** Can't connect to school network, or connected but no internet

**Quick Fixes:**

1. **Turn WiFi off and back on** - On your device, toggle airplane mode on/off or disconnect and reconnect to WiFi.

2. **Forget the network and reconnect** - Go to WiFi settings, "Forget" the school network, then reconnect and re-enter the password.

3. **Check if others have WiFi** - Ask students or a colleague. If it's widespread, it's a network issue (not your device).

4. **Restart your device** - Classic fix. Turn it off, wait 10 seconds, turn it back on.

**If none of this works:**
- Switch to offline activities (PDFs, downloaded videos, paper-based tasks)
- IT ticket: "No WiFi connection in Room [X]. Restarted device and reconnected to network. Students also affected. Need network check."

---

### Problem 4: Student Devices Won't Connect to WiFi

**Symptoms:** Your WiFi works, but student Chromebooks/iPads won't connect

**Quick Fixes:**

1. **Have students restart their devices** - 90% of the time, this fixes it.

2. **Check if they're on the right network** - Students sometimes connect to a guest network or neighboring building's WiFi.

3. **Check for profile/login issues** - Some districts require a login profile. Make sure students are logged in correctly.

4. **Try a different device** - If one device won't connect but others do, it's a device issue (not network-wide).

**If none of this works:**
- Pair struggling students with those who have working devices
- IT ticket: "Multiple student devices in Room [X] can't connect to WiFi. Network is [name]. Devices restarted and on correct network. Need support."

---

### Problem 5: LMS (Learning Management System) Won't Load

**Symptoms:** Google Classroom, Canvas, Schoology, etc. shows error or loading forever

**Quick Fixes:**

1. **Check if it's a site-wide issue** - Go to downdetector.com or ask colleagues. Sometimes the platform is down for everyone.

2. **Clear your browser cache** - Go to Settings ‚Üí Privacy ‚Üí Clear browsing data. Select "Cached images and files."

3. **Try a different browser** - If Chrome isn't working, try Edge, Safari, or Firefox.

4. **Log out and log back in** - Sometimes your session expires. Log out, close the browser, open it again, and log back in.

**If none of this works:**
- Use email or Google Drive to share resources temporarily
- IT ticket: "LMS [name] not loading in Room [X]. Cleared cache, tried different browser, and logged out/in. Need assistance."

---

### Problem 6: Sound Not Working

**Symptoms:** Video plays but no audio, or audio is too quiet

**Quick Fixes:**

1. **Check your volume** - Laptop volume AND projector/speaker volume. Both need to be up.

2. **Unmute the video** - Many videos start muted. Look for a mute icon and click it.

3. **Check audio output settings** - Your laptop might be sending audio to the wrong output (internal speakers vs. HDMI/external speakers). On Windows: Right-click sound icon ‚Üí Playback devices. On Mac: System Preferences ‚Üí Sound ‚Üí Output.

4. **Reconnect the audio cable** - If using a separate audio cable (3.5mm jack), unplug and replug it.

**If none of this works:**
- Use subtitles/closed captions if available
- IT ticket: "No audio output in Room [X]. Checked volume, output settings, and cables. Need speaker/audio troubleshooting."

---

### Problem 7: Can't Print

**Symptoms:** Printer offline, print job stuck, or nothing prints

**Quick Fixes:**

1. **Check if the printer is on** - Look for lights, paper jam, or low toner message.

2. **Cancel and resend the print job** - Go to your print queue, cancel all jobs, and send again.

3. **Restart the printer** - Turn it off, wait 10 seconds, turn it back on.

4. **Check printer connection** - If it's a network printer, make sure it's connected to WiFi. If it's USB, check the cable.

**If none of this works:**
- Print at a different location (main office, library)
- IT ticket: "Printer in Room [X] not responding. Restarted printer and resent job. Need service."

---

### Problem 8: Video Won't Play

**Symptoms:** Video buffering, error message, or frozen screen

**Quick Fixes:**

1. **Refresh the page** - Close and reopen the video. Sometimes it's just a loading glitch.

2. **Lower the video quality** - On YouTube, click the gear icon and select 480p or 360p (instead of HD). Lower quality = faster loading.

3. **Try a different browser** - If Safari isn't loading the video, try Chrome.

4. **Download the video ahead of time** - If you know WiFi is spotty, download videos before class (YouTube Premium, Vimeo, or screen record them).

**If none of this works:**
- Switch to a reading or discussion activity
- IT ticket: "Video playback issues in Room [X]. Tried different browsers and lowered quality. WiFi connection seems slow. Need network check."

---

### Problem 9: Smart Board/Interactive Display Not Responding

**Symptoms:** Touch screen not working, pen not writing, or display frozen

**Quick Fixes:**

1. **Recalibrate the touch screen** - Look for a calibration tool in the Smart Board software (usually in settings or tools menu).

2. **Check USB connection** - The Smart Board connects to your laptop via USB. Make sure it's plugged in and secure.

3. **Restart the Smart Board software** - Close the app and reopen it.

4. **Restart your laptop** - Sometimes the laptop loses connection to the board.

**If none of this works:**
- Use the board as a regular projector (still displays images, just no touch features)
- IT ticket: "Smart Board in Room [X] not responding to touch. Recalibrated, checked USB, and restarted software. Need troubleshooting."

---

### Problem 10: Student Device is Frozen/Unresponsive

**Symptoms:** Chromebook, iPad, or laptop stuck on loading screen or won't respond

**Quick Fixes:**

1. **Force restart** - Hold down the power button for 10 seconds until it shuts off. Wait 5 seconds, turn it back on.

2. **Check for updates** - Sometimes devices freeze during an update. Let it finish (this can take 10-15 minutes).

3. **Try a different student login** - If one student's profile is causing issues, have them log in on a different device to see if the problem persists.

**If none of this works:**
- Have the student share a device with a partner
- IT ticket: "Student device [ID or asset number] frozen in Room [X]. Force restarted multiple times. Need device swap or repair."

## Preventative Maintenance (Avoid Problems Before They Start)

### Weekly Checks:
- **Test your projector** on Monday morning before students arrive
- **Download key videos** ahead of time (don't rely on WiFi)
- **Charge all devices** (laptop, document camera, clicker) over the weekend

---

### Monthly Checks:
- **Update your laptop** - Run software updates during planning periods (not during class!)
- **Clean your tech** - Wipe down your keyboard, mouse, and projector remote
- **Check cables** - Look for fraying or loose connections

---

### Start of Year:
- **Submit a preemptive IT ticket** - Ask IT to check all equipment in your room before school starts
- **Request backup equipment** - Ask for a spare HDMI cable, VGA adapter, or extension cord to keep in your room

---

## Tech Fail Backup Activities (No WiFi/Tech Needed)

Keep these ideas in your back pocket:

1. **Think-Pair-Share** - Pose a question, students discuss in pairs, share out
2. **Whiteboard Practice** - Write problems/prompts on the board, students solve in notebooks
3. **Socratic Seminar** - Text-based discussion (print the text ahead of time)
4. **Gallery Walk** - Post chart paper around the room, students rotate and respond
5. **Exit Ticket** - Quick written reflection on today's topic (paper + pencil)

## When to Call for Help (vs. Troubleshoot Yourself)

### DIY It:
- Loose cables
- Volume issues
- Display input settings
- Simple restarts

### Call IT:
- Network outages
- Broken hardware (cracked screen, dead battery)
- Software installation/licensing issues
- Security/login problems

**Rule of Thumb:** If you've tried 3 fixes and it's still not working, call IT. Don't waste 30 minutes of class time troubleshooting.

## FAQ

**Q: What if IT takes days to respond?**  
Escalate to your admin. If it's impacting your ability to teach, it's urgent. Also, use the template above to be specific - vague tickets get slower responses.

**Q: What if students break devices on purpose?**  
Document it (date, time, what happened) and report to admin. This is a behavior issue, not just a tech issue.

**Q: What if I don't know the model/brand of my equipment?**  
Take a photo and include it in your IT ticket. Or describe it: "ceiling-mounted projector in Room 204."

**Q: What if I'm scared of breaking something by trying to fix it?**  
You won't. Unplugging a cable, restarting a device, or checking settings won't cause damage. If you're unsure, ask IT - but most "fixes" are safe.
