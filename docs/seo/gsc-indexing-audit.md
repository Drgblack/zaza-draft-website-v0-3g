# GSC Indexing Audit

Generated after the March 12, 2026 indexing cleanup pass.

## Intent

Zaza Draft should only expose canonical, public, index-worthy pages to Google. Redirecting legacy aliases, canonicalised duplicate families, and low-confidence matrix pages should stay available only where they serve users, not as competing index candidates.

## What Changed In This Pass

1. Added permanent redirect coverage for legacy aliases and helper URLs in `next.config.mjs`.
2. Switched redirect-only alias page components from temporary redirects to permanent redirects where needed.
3. Fixed the stale `/state-of-ai-report` footer link.
4. Expanded `scripts/link-audit.ts` to scan `lib/` so config-driven stale links are caught.
5. Added explicit canonical and alternate metadata to dynamic detail templates that previously had weak or missing canonical signals.
6. Tightened sitemap filtering so canonical-to-hub alternate URLs are excluded from both sitemap tiers.
7. Added `scripts/seo-index-guard.ts` to fail when sitemap URLs are missing, redirected, non-canonical, or non-indexable.

## Automated Checks

- `pnpm exec tsx scripts/link-audit.ts --dry-run`
  - Confirms internal links in `app`, `components`, `content`, and `lib` do not point at redirect or 404 targets.
- `pnpm exec tsx scripts/seo-index-guard.ts`
  - Confirms sitemap URLs are route-valid, canonical-to-self, non-redirecting, and indexable under current rules.

## Risks Still Worth Watching

- Some DE detail routes still mirror EN content closely. Canonical and hreflang are now clearer, but GSC may still prefer only one locale where content differentiation is minimal.
- The longtail inventory is much cleaner, but promoted programmatic cohorts should still be reviewed against impressions and indexing behaviour in GSC.
- Historical 404s may remain in GSC for a while even after redirects are in place. That is normal until Google recrawls and validates the fixes.
