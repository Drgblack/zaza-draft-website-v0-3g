# Programmatic SEO Guardrails

## Minimum content quality

- Do not publish any page below 900 useful words
- Target 1,050 to 1,350 words for most route families
- Every page must include a direct answer near the top, not a padded introduction
- Every page must include 4 to 6 editable examples or equivalent practical guidance
- Every page must include 6 to 8 FAQs
- Every page must include at least 6 internal links including the hub

## Tone and brand

- Write for exhausted teachers, not marketers
- Use UK English throughout
- Keep the tone calm, professional, emotionally intelligent, and low-hype
- Reinforce that Zaza Draft is a co-writer, not a replacement
- Make it clear that teachers edit and approve every final word
- Avoid generic AI language such as revolutionary, game-changing, magic, or transform

## Safety and trust

- Never invent pupil facts, parent motives, or school actions
- Never imply automatic sending, automatic reporting, or automatic school decisions
- Keep all claims conservative and review-led
- If a page references safeguarding, SEN, SLT, Ofsted, or parents' evening, do so naturally and only when context supports it
- Do not add testimonials unless they are verified
- Comparison pages must stay factual and fit-based

## SEO quality rules

- Put the target keyword in the title tag, H1, first screen copy, at least one H2, and the meta description
- Use related teacher terms naturally, not mechanically
- Reject token-swapped combinations that do not create a meaningfully different wording problem
- Do not create multiple near-identical pages that differ only by a weak modifier
- Keep internal linking purposeful. Do not link for the sake of link count

## Editorial review checklist

- Does the page solve a real teacher wording problem?
- Does the hero sound like a teacher problem rather than a marketing pitch?
- Are the examples plausible, proportionate, and easy to edit?
- Is teacher control explicit in the hero, trust section, and CTA?
- Would the page still feel useful if the CTA were removed?
- Would a school leader find the language proportionate and safe?

## Publishing rules

- Publish seed routes first, then expand based on impressions and trial-start data
- Rework underperforming templates before scaling the family further
- Revalidate changed hubs and seed routes whenever the shared template or core examples change
- Keep future-category routes out of the main push until they show distinct demand
